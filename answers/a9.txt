Title: Investigating the Impact of Urban Green Spaces on Mental Health and Wellbeing in Diverse Urban Populations

Background:
Urbanization has become a pressing issue worldwide, with millions of people living in densely populated cities. While urbanization offers numerous economic and social benefits, it also poses significant challenges to human health and wellbeing. Urban green spaces have been identified as a potential solution to mitigate the negative effects of urbanization on mental health.

Objective:
This study aims to investigate the relationship between urban green spaces and mental health and wellbeing in diverse urban populations, with a focus on understanding the specific benefits and mechanisms underlying this association.

Methods:
This is a mixed-methods study that combines quantitative and qualitative approaches. A survey was conducted among 1,000 participants from three urban cities (n=333 per city) to collect data on their exposure to green spaces, mental health symptoms (e.g., anxiety, depression), and demographic characteristics. In addition, in-depth interviews were conducted with 30 residents from each city to explore the subjective experiences and meanings associated with green space use.

Results:
The results show that individuals who spent more time in urban green spaces reported lower levels of anxiety and depression, as well as improved sleep quality and overall wellbeing. The qualitative findings revealed that participants perceived green spaces as a source of comfort, relaxation, and social connection, which helped to mitigate the negative effects of urbanization on mental health.

Conclusion:
This study provides evidence for the positive impact of urban green spaces on mental health and wellbeing in diverse urban populations. The findings highlight the importance of incorporating green spaces into urban planning and design, particularly in densely populated cities, to promote public health and wellbeing. Furthermore, this study underscores the need to consider the subjective experiences and meanings associated with green space use when designing and implementing urban green infrastructure.
